Ashwin Vadivel Master's Student Georgia Tech (remote)

Ashwin Vadivel earned bachelor’s and master’s degrees in electrical engineering from Georgia Tech and Florida State University (FSU) respectively. At FSU, he worked as a research assistant in the Resilient and Autonomous Systems Lab under Dr. Olugbenga Moses Anubi investigating perception methods for autonomous robots. He later worked in industry as a software engineer on the autonomous driving team at Qualcomm and on data center video accelerator products at AMD. He is currently pursuing an MS in Computer Science, serves as a part-time instructor in electronics and programming at Aims Community College, and is a research intern in the NeCoLe Lab at CU Anschutz (Georgia Tech’s faculty sponsor: Nabil Imam), where his research interests center on exploring the computational and mechanistic basis of adaptive behavior in biological systems under real-world constraints. Outside the lab, he enjoys maintaining a large saltwater aquarium, hiking and exploring Colorado, and spending time with his dog and cat.
